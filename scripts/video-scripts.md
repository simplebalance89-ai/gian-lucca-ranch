# Story Time Video Scripts

Each script is written for a calm, slow bedtime-story pace, targeting roughly 2 to 2.5 minutes of narration.

## 1. The Sleepy Little Bear

Once upon a time, in the coziest corner of Gian Lucca's Ranch, there lived a little bear named Benny. Benny had the softest brown fur you have ever felt, two round button eyes, and a yawn so big it could blow the leaves right off a tree. But tonight, even though the moon was already high in the sky and the crickets were singing their very best sleepy song, Benny just could not fall asleep.

Benny fluffed his pillow three times. He counted sheep until his toes got tired. He even tried standing on one paw and humming a lullaby to himself. But his eyes stayed wide, wide open.

So Benny tiptoed out of his little den and into the moonlit meadow. The grass felt cool and tickly beneath his paws. Fireflies blinked on and off like tiny lanterns guiding his way. The wind whispered secrets through the tall oak trees, and somewhere in the distance, a brook bubbled a soft, bubbling tune.

Benny looked up and saw Old Owl perched on a low branch. "Owl," Benny whispered, "how do you fall asleep? I have tried everything."

Old Owl blinked his big golden eyes and hooted gently. "I close my eyes, little Benny, and I listen. I listen to the whole ranch settling down for the night."

Benny sat very still in the soft grass. He listened. He heard the brook bubbling over smooth stones. He heard the horses breathing softly in the barn. He heard the leaves rustling like a quiet lullaby. And far, far away, he heard the sound of Daddy's voice, humming a slow and sweet goodnight song.

Benny's eyelids began to feel heavy. His paws felt warm and fuzzy. He curled up right there in the meadow, and before the very next firefly blinked, Benny the little bear was fast asleep. He dreamed of honey, of warm hugs, and of another beautiful day on Gian Lucca's Ranch.

Goodnight, little one. Sweet dreams.

## 2. Benny Bear's Adventure

Benny the Bear woke up with a sparkle in his eyes. Today was the day for an adventure. The sun was peeking over the meadow, painting everything gold and pink. The birds were chirping the adventure song, and the whole ranch was waiting to be explored.

Benny packed his tiny backpack very carefully. Inside he put three important things: a shiny red apple for energy, his favorite blue bandana for bravery, and a map he had drawn himself. The map had a big, bold X right in the middle of the meadow. "Treasure!" Benny whispered, and off he went.

First, Benny passed the chicken coop. The Chicken Crew was clucking and pecking at their breakfast. "Where are you going, Benny?" they clucked curiously.

"On a treasure hunt!" Benny replied with a proud smile.

"Good luck!" the chickens cheered, flapping their wings.

Next, Benny trotted past the turkey run. Tommy Turkey was practicing his most proud strut. "Need a marching buddy?" Tommy gobbled.

"Sure!" said Benny, and the two friends marched side by side, humming a brave little tune.

At the meadow, Benny dug right where the X was marked. His paws hit something hard. With one big pull, he uncovered an old wooden box. Inside was not gold, and not jewels, but something even better. It was a stack of handwritten notes from Daddy. Each one said, "I love you, Benny. I love you, Gianluca. I love our ranch."

Benny's heart felt full and warm. He looked at Tommy and smiled. "This is the best treasure ever," he said.

And together, they sat in the warm sunshine, sharing the apple and knowing that love is the greatest adventure of all.

The end.

## 3. Chicken Dance Party

Have you ever seen a chicken dance? Well, on Gian Lucca's Ranch, the chickens know all the best moves, and today they were throwing the biggest dance party the barn had ever seen.

Cluck Norris, the rooster with the shiniest red comb, turned up the music. Bawkstreet, the smallest little chick, blew up colorful balloons. And Feathers McGee tied tiny bells around her ankles so every step went jingle-jangle, jingle-jangle.

The barn doors swung wide open. The sun poured in like a golden spotlight. "Ladies and chickens!" Cluck Norris crowed. "Let the dance party begin!"

First came the Funky Flap. Wings went up, wings went down, and everyone spun in circles until they were dizzy and giggling.

Next was the Peck and Shuffle. Heads bobbed, feet shuffled, and the bells went jingle-jangle-jingle all around the barn.

Then Benny Bear wandered in. "Can I dance too?" he asked.

"Of course!" clucked Feathers McGee. "Everyone can dance on Gian Lucca's Ranch!"

Benny tried the Funky Flap, but his paws got a little tangled. He tried the Peck and Shuffle, but he almost tripped on his own fuzzy ears. The chickens giggled kindly. Then Benny did his very own move: the Big Bear Bounce. Boing, boing, boing! The whole barn shook with laughter.

Soon Tommy Turkey joined in, then the goats, then even the cows mooed along to the beat. The dance party lasted until the sun turned pink and Daddy called everyone in for supper.

As the chickens settled into their cozy coop, Feathers McGee whispered, "Best. Party. Ever."

And Benny, still bouncing a little, whispered back, "Same time tomorrow?"

Keep dancing, little one.

## 4. Tommy Turkey's Day

Tommy Turkey woke up early on Gian Lucca's Ranch. He puffed out his feathers, stretched his wings wide, and took a deep breath of the fresh morning air. Today, Tommy wanted to do something very special. Today, he wanted to say thank you.

Tommy waddled to the chicken coop first. "Thank you for sharing your corn with me yesterday," he gobbled politely.

The chickens clucked happily. "You are very welcome, Tommy!"

Then Tommy found Benny Bear sitting under the big oak tree. "Thank you for helping me reach the shiny apples," Tommy said.

Benny smiled. "Friends help friends," he replied.

At the barn, Tommy saw Whiskers the ranch cat curled up on a hay bale. "Thank you for keeping the mice away so we can sleep snug and safe," Tommy said.

Whiskers purred and tipped her tail.

By afternoon, Tommy had thanked almost everyone on the ranch. His heart felt warm and full, like a little cup of hot cocoa on a cold day. But there was still one more thank-you he needed to give.

Tommy waddled up the gentle hill where Daddy was fixing a wooden fence. "Daddy," Tommy said, "thank you for taking care of all of us. Thank you for the food, the hugs, and the bedtime stories."

Daddy knelt down and gently patted Tommy's feathers. "Thank you for being you, Tommy," Daddy said. "You make this ranch a kinder, happier place."

That night, as the stars came out one by one, Tommy Turkey fell asleep feeling grateful, loved, and very proud to be part of Gian Lucca's Ranch.

What are you thankful for today?

## 5. Ranch Morning Routine

Good morning, sunshine. On Gian Lucca's Ranch, every morning starts with a happy song.

The very first sound is Roo the Rooster. "Cock-a-doodle-doo!" he calls from the fence post. "Time to wake up, sleepy ranch!"

Benny Bear stretches in his cozy den and yawns a great big bear yawn. Tommy Turkey puffs out his feathers and does three proud laps around the turkey run. The Chicken Crew hops off their roost, ready for breakfast and, of course, a little morning dance.

Daddy is already up. He fills the water buckets, scoops fresh grain into little bowls, and hums a tune that makes every animal feel safe and loved. The smell of warm hay floats through the air, and the sun paints the meadow the color of golden honey.

"Good morning, Benny!" Daddy calls.

"Good morning, Tommy!"

"Good morning, Chicken Crew!"

Each animal answers in its own special way: a grunt, a gobble, a cluck, and a happy little bounce.

After breakfast, Benny helps Daddy check the fences. Tommy follows along, pecking at shiny stones. The chickens scratch in the garden, looking for wiggly bugs, because bugs help keep the plants healthy and happy.

By mid-morning, the ranch is bright and busy and full of love. Daddy sits on the porch with a cup of something warm, and Benny curls up right at his feet.

"This is the best ranch in the whole wide world," Benny whispers.

Daddy smiles. "Yes, it is," he says. "Because we are all here together."

Good morning, little rancher.

## 6. Starlight Lullaby

Close your eyes and imagine the biggest, bluest sky you have ever seen. Now picture it slowly turning pink, then purple, then deep velvet blue. The stars begin to twinkle one by one, like tiny night-lights switched on just for you.

On Gian Lucca's Ranch, the stars have names. The brightest one above the barn is called Hugs. The little cluster near the meadow is called Giggles. And the slow, sleepy star that always blinks last is called Sweet Dreams.

Benny Bear is already tucked into his den, wrapped in a blanket of soft moss. Tommy Turkey rests his head beneath one warm wing. The Chicken Crew cuddles close, feather to feather, whispering quiet clucks to each other.

A gentle wind blows through the oak trees, and it sounds like the ranch is humming a lullaby. Shhhhhh, shhhhhh, shhhhhh.

Daddy walks from window to window, turning off the lights and pulling the curtains. "Goodnight, ranch," he whispers. "Goodnight, Benny. Goodnight, Tommy. Goodnight, Chicken Crew. Goodnight, Gianluca. I love you all the way to the stars and back."

High above, the stars twinkle a little brighter, as if they are answering back, "We love you too."

So close your eyes, little one. Let the starlight wrap around you like a soft, silver blanket. Let your breath slow down. Let your dreams be sweet.

Tomorrow the sun will rise, the rooster will crow, and another beautiful day on Gian Lucca's Ranch will begin. But for now, it is time to rest.

Goodnight. I love you. Sweet dreams.
